# Decision Framework

Every significant proposal must answer these questions before it can be approved. Incomplete answers
are themselves a finding — see [failure-handling.md](failure-handling.md).

## Problem

- What problem exists?
- How severe is it?
- Who experiences it?

## Customer

- Who benefits?
- How frequently?
- How urgently?

## Solution

- How does the solution remove the pain?
- Why is it better?

## Technology

- Can it actually be built?
- Can it scale?
- Can it be maintained?

## Business

- Can customers pay?
- How much?
- How often?

## Competition

- Who already solves this?
- What are their weaknesses?
- What is our advantage?

## Risks

Technical · Financial · Legal · Operational · Security · Market.

## Success Metrics

Revenue · Retention · Adoption · Reliability · Performance · Customer Satisfaction.

---

## Reversible vs. irreversible decisions

- **Reversible ("two-way door"):** decide quickly at the lowest responsible level.
- **Irreversible ("one-way door"):** require cross-functional review and CEO/human approval via the
  [approval engine](../orchestration/approval-engine.md).

The result of a decision is recorded to [decision memory](../memory/decision-memory.md).
