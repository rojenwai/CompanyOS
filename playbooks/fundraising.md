# Playbook: Fundraising

**When to use:** capital would accelerate a working, defensible model - or runway needs extending.

1. **Decide whether to raise** - model runway and the raise-vs-bootstrap trade ([unit economics](../strategy/unit-economics.md)); raise to accelerate a working model, not to fund an unproven one.
2. **Get the numbers right** - a clean financial model, reconciled metrics, and a current [cap table](../agents/investor-relations/cap-table-agent.md) ([finance](../finance/README.md)).
3. **Sharpen the narrative** - problem, traction, market, why-now, use of funds ([strategy](../strategy/README.md)); the [Fundraising Agent](../agents/strategy/fundraising-agent.md) assembles the pack.
4. **Prepare the data room** - metrics, financials, legal, and traction ready for diligence ([investor-relations](../investor-relations/README.md)).
5. **Run the process** - build a pipeline of investors; run it tightly; keep momentum.
6. **Model dilution** - understand each term's impact ([cap table](../agents/investor-relations/cap-table-agent.md)); commitments require [CEO/CFO approval](../orchestration/approval-engine.md).
7. **Close and communicate** - finalize with [legal](../legal/README.md); update the board and investors.

**Success criteria:** funded on good terms - or a deliberate decision to extend runway instead.
**Escalate if:** runway drops below threshold mid-process → the [runway playbook](../finance/playbooks.md).
