# Playbook: Validating an Idea

**When to use:** you have a shortlisted idea and want to know if it is worth building - before spending
months on it. Full method in [strategy/idea-validation.md](../strategy/idea-validation.md).

1. **List assumptions and rank by risk** - what must be true, and how fatal if it is false? Test the riskiest first.
2. **Write kill criteria in advance** - the specific result that would drop or pivot the idea (decide before you see the data).
3. **Climb the validation ladder only as far as evidence justifies:**
   - Desk research → problem [interviews](../product/discovery.md) → landing page / smoke test → fake door → concierge / Wizard-of-Oz → pre-sales / LOIs → paid pilot.
4. **Use the [Mom Test](../strategy/idea-validation.md)** - ask about their life and past behavior, not about your idea.
5. **Weigh real signals over flattering ones** - people pay, use repeatedly, and refer; ignore compliments and vanity sign-ups.
6. **Decide** - pursue → [define the product](../product/requirements.md), pivot, or drop, with the evidence recorded in [research memory](../memory/research-memory.md).

**Success criteria:** a validated (or invalidated) problem with real evidence and a clear decision.
**Next (if validated):** [building-an-mvp.md](building-an-mvp.md). **Escalate if:** evidence is ambiguous → run one more cheaper test, do not build to "find out."
