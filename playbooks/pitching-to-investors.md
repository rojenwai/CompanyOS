# Playbook: Pitching to Investors

**When to use:** you have decided to [raise](fundraising.md) and need to pitch. Full guidance in
[strategy/pitching.md](../strategy/pitching.md) and [where to find investors](../strategy/finding-investors.md).

1. **Sharpen the one-liner** - "We help [who] achieve [outcome] by [how], unlike [alternative]" ([pitching.md](../strategy/pitching.md#the-one-sentence-pitch-lead-with-this)).
2. **Build the deck** - 10-12 slides following the [narrative arc](../strategy/pitching.md#the-investor-pitch-deck-10-12-slides): problem → why now → solution → market → model → traction → team → ask.
3. **Know your numbers cold** - metrics reconcile to [finance](../finance/README.md); model your [unit economics](../strategy/unit-economics.md).
4. **Line up the right investors** - match [source to stage](../strategy/finding-investors.md) and get warm intros.
5. **Prepare a tailored [demo](../strategy/pitching.md#the-demo)** and a recorded backup.
6. **Rehearse Q&A** - prepare honest answers to your three biggest weaknesses; never bluff ([failure-handling](../governance/failure-handling.md)).
7. **Run it as a pipeline** - parallel conversations create genuine urgency ([like sales](../sales/methodology.md)); commitments need [CEO/CFO approval](../orchestration/approval-engine.md).

**Success criteria:** a clear, confident pitch that tells a true story and makes a specific ask.
**Next:** the broader [fundraising playbook](fundraising.md); keep investors updated via [investor-relations](../investor-relations/README.md).
