# Product Workflows — Product Lifecycle

Every product moves through this lifecycle. Product owns the front (discovery → launch) and the
loop back (analytics → iteration); [post-launch](../post-launch/README.md) owns operations and
[retirement](../post-launch/end-of-life.md).

```
Problem Discovery → Customer Research → Market Validation → Product Vision → Requirements
→ UX Research → Design → Development → Testing → Launch → Analytics → Iteration
→ Scaling → Retirement
```

## Core workflows

### Discovery → definition
1. Discover and validate the problem ([discovery.md](discovery.md)).
2. Define personas ([user-personas.md](user-personas.md)) and the journey ([customer-journey.md](customer-journey.md)).
3. Write the [PRD and requirements](requirements.md).
4. Prioritize ([decision-frameworks.md](decision-frameworks.md)) and scope the [MVP](../agents/product/mvp-planning-agent.md).

### Definition → delivery
1. Break requirements into [user stories](requirements.md) that meet the [Definition of Ready](../engineering/definition-of-ready.md).
2. Hand to [engineering](../engineering/README.md) and [design](../design/README.md).
3. Track on the [roadmap and backlog](roadmap.md); plan the [release](roadmap.md).

### Launch → learn
1. Pass the [launch checklist](quality-gates.md).
2. Measure with [analytics](analytics.md) and [metrics](metrics.md).
3. Feed learnings into the [iteration loop](../post-launch/README.md).

This lifecycle runs on top of the company [SDLC](../workflows/sdlc.md).
