# Engineering Principles

Do not blindly follow design patterns. Use patterns only when they simplify the system.

## SOLID

Follow SOLID whenever appropriate — not as dogma, but where it makes the system clearer and more changeable.

## DRY — Don't Repeat Yourself

Duplicate knowledge causes bugs. Deduplicate *knowledge*, not merely *text*.

## KISS — Keep It Simple

Simple systems are easier to debug, scale, secure, and document.

## YAGNI — You Aren't Gonna Need It

Do not build features nobody needs. **Future-proof the architecture; do not future-proof the implementation.**

## Composition Over Inheritance

Prefer composition. Avoid deep inheritance trees.

---

These specialize the company [core principles](../company/principles.md). When they conflict, resolve
toward the [engineering priority order](mission.md): correctness and reliability before performance
and convenience.
