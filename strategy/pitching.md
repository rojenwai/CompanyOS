# How to Pitch

A pitch is a **story that makes someone believe** - an investor that this is a fundable business, or a
customer that this solves their problem. Same core story, different emphasis. A great idea pitched badly
loses; a good idea pitched with clarity and conviction wins. Pitching is a learnable skill.

## The one-sentence pitch (lead with this)

> We help **[specific who]** achieve **[specific outcome]** by **[how]**, unlike **[the alternative]**.

If you cannot say it in one clear sentence, the idea is not yet sharp enough. Build the rest of the pitch
on this spine. (See [positioning](../marketing/README.md).)

## The narrative arc

People remember stories, not slides. Structure the pitch as a story:

1. **The problem** - open with a real, specific customer feeling real pain. Make the listener feel it.
2. **Why now** - what changed (technology, behavior, regulation) that makes this the right moment.
3. **The solution** - your answer, ideally shown, not told (a [demo](#the-demo) beats adjectives).
4. **Why you win** - what makes this defensible and hard to copy ([defensibility](README.md)).
5. **Traction** - evidence it is working (users, revenue, retention, [validation](idea-validation.md)).
6. **The market** - how big this can become ([TAM/SAM/SOM](market-analysis.md)).
7. **Business model** - how you make money ([business-model.md](business-model.md)).
8. **The team** - why *you* are the ones to build this.
9. **The ask** - exactly what you want (amount, and what it unlocks).

## The investor pitch deck (10-12 slides)

| Slide | Content |
|---|---|
| 1. Title | Company, one-line pitch |
| 2. Problem | The pain, made concrete |
| 3. Solution | Your approach |
| 4. Why now | The enabling shift |
| 5. Product | Demo or screenshots |
| 6. Market | TAM/SAM/SOM, sourced ([market-analysis.md](market-analysis.md)) |
| 7. Business model | Pricing and unit economics ([unit-economics.md](unit-economics.md)) |
| 8. Traction | Growth, retention, revenue |
| 9. Competition | Landscape and your edge |
| 10. Team | Why this team wins |
| 11. Financials | Forecast and key metrics ([finance](../finance/README.md)) |
| 12. The ask | Raise amount, use of funds, milestones it buys |

Keep it visual and sparse - the deck supports you, it is not the script.

## The demo

For product pitches, **show, don't tell**. A demo tailored to the listener's actual problem beats any
feature list. Rehearse it; have a recorded backup; never demo something fragile live without a fallback.

## Delivery

- **Confidence and clarity.** Believe it, say it simply, cut every unnecessary word.
- **Know your numbers cold.** Nothing erodes trust faster than fumbling your own metrics.
- **Anticipate the hard questions.** Prepare answers to your three biggest weaknesses ([Q&A](#handling-qa)).
- **Brevity.** Say the most important thing first; you may not get to slide 12.

## Handling Q&A

Questions are interest, not attack. Answer directly and honestly; if you do not know, say so and follow up
- never bluff (this mirrors the company rule on [honesty](../governance/failure-handling.md)). Address
weaknesses head-on; investors and customers trust founders who are candid about risk.

## Common mistakes

- Too much product, too little problem and market.
- No clear, specific ask.
- Hiding weaknesses (they surface in diligence anyway - candor builds trust).
- Reading the slides. Tell the story; the slides follow.
