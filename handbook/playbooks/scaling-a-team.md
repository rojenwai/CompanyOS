# Playbook: Scaling a Team

**When to use:** demand is outrunning capacity and you're considering growing headcount.

1. **Confirm you should scale at all** - scale only after product-market fit, repeatable acquisition, and healthy [unit economics](../departments/strategy/unit-economics.md). Scaling a broken model multiplies the breakage ([scaling readiness](../departments/strategy/checklists/scaling-readiness-checklist.md)).
2. **Diagnose the real constraint** - is it people, process, tooling, or focus? More people cannot fix an unclear priority or a broken process ([process optimization](../departments/operations/README.md)).
3. **Consider leverage before headcount** - automation, agents, and removing work often beat hiring. Hiring is the slowest, most expensive lever.
4. **Protect talent density** - hire on evidence against a [scorecard](../departments/hr/examples/scorecard-example.md); a small great team beats a large average one ([hr principles](../departments/hr/principles.md)).
5. **Fund and sequence the hires** - approve headcount with [finance](../departments/finance/README.md); hire the constraint first, not the wish list.
6. **Scale onboarding with hiring** - every role needs a [30-day path](../guides/onboarding-paths.md) *before* the hire starts, or new people dilute the team instead of adding to it.
7. **Add structure only as needed** - split teams when coordination cost exceeds the benefit; keep ownership clear and cadences intact ([operations](../departments/operations/README.md)).
8. **Watch the culture** - growth is where culture erodes. Monitor engagement and reinforce [principles](../company/principles.md) deliberately ([culture agent](../../ai/agents/hr/culture-agent.md)).

**Success criteria:** capacity grows without falling talent density, slower delivery, or cultural drift.
**Escalate if:** delivery slows as headcount rises → the constraint was never people; stop hiring and fix the real bottleneck.
