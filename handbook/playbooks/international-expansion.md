# Playbook: International Expansion

**When to use:** the business works in its home market and you're considering entering another country.

1. **Earn the right to expand** - a repeatable sales motion, healthy [unit economics](../departments/strategy/unit-economics.md), stable infrastructure, and positive retention at home. Expansion multiplies problems as readily as revenue.
2. **Pick one market, deliberately** - size the opportunity ([market analysis](../departments/strategy/market-analysis.md)), assess competition, and check founder/team advantage. One market done well beats five done thinly.
3. **Validate demand locally** - the problem, willingness to pay, and buying process differ by market. Re-run cheap [validation](../departments/strategy/idea-validation.md); do not assume the home thesis transfers.
4. **Clear the legal and regulatory path first** - entity, taxation, employment law, data-protection and privacy regimes, contracts ([legal](../departments/legal/README.md), [compliance](../departments/security/README.md)). This is the step that most often blocks or delays; start it early.
5. **Localize the product** - language, currency, payment methods, date/number formats, [accessibility](../departments/design/accessibility.md), and data residency ([data governance](../../ai/agents/data/data-governance-agent.md)).
6. **Localize the go-to-market** - positioning, channels, and pricing rarely translate directly ([GTM](../departments/strategy/go-to-market.md), [channels](../departments/sales/channels.md)).
7. **Stand up local operations** - support hours and language, [on-call](../departments/post-launch/on-call.md) coverage across time zones, infrastructure regions, and partnerships.
8. **Instrument and review** - track the new market's CAC, retention, and margin separately ([finance](../departments/finance/README.md)). Give it a fair trial with pre-set success and exit criteria.

**Success criteria:** the new market reaches viable unit economics on its own, without degrading the home market.
**Escalate if:** legal/regulatory exposure is unresolved → stop; or the market misses its criteria → exit gracefully rather than subsidize indefinitely.
