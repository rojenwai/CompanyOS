# Example - Interview Debrief

> Worked example - illustrative, not a live record. Evidence is written **before** the group discusses, so
> the first opinion doesn't anchor the rest. Evaluated against the
> [role scorecard](scorecard-example.md); process in the [hiring checklist](../checklists/hiring-checklist.md).

**Candidate:** A. Candidate · **Role:** Senior Backend Engineer · **Loop:** 4 interviewers

## Written feedback (submitted independently, before the debrief)

| Competency | Interviewer | Evidence observed | Rating |
|---|---|---|---|
| System design | Architect | Designed a queue-backed job system; unprompted, raised at-least-once delivery and idempotency. Chose a managed service and justified it on operational cost. | Strong hire |
| Code quality | Backend Eng | Clean, tested solution. Wrote the failing test first. Named the edge case (empty input) before being asked. | Hire |
| Ownership | Eng Manager | Described driving an incident end-to-end at a previous company, including the postmortem and the debt it created. Owned a mistake without deflecting. | Strong hire |
| Collaboration | Frontend Eng | Explained trade-offs clearly to a non-backend audience. Asked good questions. Slightly dismissive of a junior's alternative approach when pushed. | Hire (with note) |

## Debrief

Discussion focused on the one flag: the dismissiveness in the collaboration interview. Two interviewers had
observed generous, patient explanation; one had not. Probed with a reference check on how the candidate
mentors juniors - the reference described specific, concrete examples of levelling up two engineers.

**Conclusion:** the flag was a single moment under time pressure, not a pattern.

## Decision

**Hire.** Clears the bar on all four competencies with evidence, and is bar-raising on system design and
ownership - the two the role most needs ([scorecard](scorecard-example.md)).

**Onboarding note for the manager:** pair the candidate with a junior early; make mentoring an explicit
first-90-days expectation ([onboarding path](../../../guides/onboarding-paths.md)).

---

## Why this debrief works

- **Evidence, not impressions.** Every rating cites something the candidate actually did or said.
- **The flag was investigated**, not dismissed *or* over-weighted.
- **The decision maps to the scorecard**, not to whether people liked the candidate.
- **A "hire" produced a concrete onboarding action** - the loop teaches you how to manage them well.
