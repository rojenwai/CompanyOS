# Research Framework

Every research project follows the same process.

```
Question → Background Research → Evidence Collection → Analysis
→ Hypothesis → Validation → Experiment → Results → Decision
```

## Workflow

1. **Define the research question** — specific and answerable. Bad: "Learn AI." Good: "How can
   vision-language models reduce inspection costs in manufacturing?"
2. **Collect evidence** — never a single source: papers, products, competitors, standards, benchmarks, datasets, patents.
3. **Organize** — each document records summary · key findings · evidence · limitations · future work · references.
4. **Generate hypotheses** — e.g. "Edge AI + satellite imagery cuts agricultural monitoring cost 80%."
5. **Design experiments** — objective · variables · method · dataset · metrics · expected result · failure conditions.
6. **Validate** — if evidence disproves the hypothesis, discard it. Never defend incorrect assumptions.

## Quality standards

Every report answers: What problem exists? Why does it matter? Who is affected? Current solutions and
their limitations? The opportunity? Technical and commercial feasibility? Future impact?

## Experimentation & failure policy

Every experiment defines success **and** failure criteria, cost, timeline, and a decision rule. Failed
experiments are valuable: document why it failed, lessons learned, and unexpected discoveries. **Failure
without learning is unacceptable** — lessons go to [lessons-learned](../../../ai/memory/lessons-learned.md).
