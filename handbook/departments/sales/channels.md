# Where to Sell - Channels & Motions

*How* you sell must match *what* you sell and *who* buys it. A $20/month product cannot afford a
field-sales rep; a $200k enterprise deal will not close itself through a sign-up button. This page maps
the sales motions and where your buyers actually are. It complements the
[go-to-market strategy](../strategy/go-to-market.md).

## Match the motion to the price (ACV)

| Annual contract value | Motion | How it works |
|---|---|---|
| **< ~$1k** | **Product-led / self-serve** | The product sells itself: free trial or freemium, in-product upgrade, no human in the loop |
| **~$1k-$25k** | **Inside sales** | Remote reps; [SDR](../../../ai/agents/sales/prospecting-agent.md) qualifies, [AE](../../../ai/agents/sales/account-executive-agent.md) closes over calls |
| **> ~$25k** | **Field / enterprise sales** | High-touch: discovery, [solution engineering](../../../ai/agents/sales/sales-engineer-agent.md), security review, procurement, multiple stakeholders |
| **Any** | **Channel / partner** | Resellers, system integrators, and referral partners sell on your behalf |

Rule of thumb: the higher the price and the more stakeholders involved, the more human touch the motion
needs. Mismatches burn money (too much touch for a cheap product) or leave money on the table (too little
for an expensive one).

## The motions in depth

- **Product-led growth (PLG).** The product is the primary acquisition and conversion engine. Users try
  free, get value, and upgrade themselves. Best for low ACV, high volume, and fast time-to-value. Requires
  an exceptional [onboarding-to-value](../customer-success/README.md) experience.
- **Inside sales.** A repeatable, remote motion: prospect → qualify → demo → close over video/phone.
  Scales with hired reps and a documented [methodology](methodology.md).
- **Enterprise / field sales.** Long cycles, large deals, many stakeholders, security and procurement
  gates. Fits the [enterprise-b2b starter kit](../../../starter-kits/enterprise-b2b/README.md).
- **Channel / partner.** Others sell for you - resellers, integrators, marketplaces, and referral
  partners. Extends reach but shares margin and requires partner enablement.

## Where to sell - the places, not just the motion

Go where your buyers already gather:

- **Cloud marketplaces.** AWS / Azure / Google Cloud marketplaces - powerful for B2B software, since
  buyers can purchase against existing cloud commitments.
- **App stores & plugin marketplaces.** Mobile app stores, SaaS app directories (e.g. Salesforce,
  Shopify, Slack, Atlassian), browser-extension and plugin stores - distribution where the platform's
  users already are.
- **Communities.** For [developer tools](../../../starter-kits/developer-tools/README.md) especially, developer
  communities, forums, and open-source ecosystems are the channel.
- **Search & content.** Buyers researching a problem find you through [SEO and content](../marketing/README.md).
- **Events & industry channels.** Conferences, trade shows, and vertical communities for high-touch B2B.
- **Direct outbound.** Targeted [prospecting](../../../ai/agents/sales/prospecting-agent.md) into a well-defined ICP.

## Inbound vs. outbound

- **Inbound** - buyers come to you (content, SEO, community, marketplaces, word of mouth). Lower cost per
  lead, slower to start, compounds over time.
- **Outbound** - you go to buyers (prospecting, ads, events). Faster to start, higher cost, essential for
  enterprise and for early founder-led selling.

Most companies blend both. Prove the economics of a channel before scaling spend into it
([growth](../marketing/README.md), [unit economics](../strategy/unit-economics.md)).

## Choosing

Start where your specific buyer already is and where the motion matches your price. Do not spread across
every channel at once - win one channel with viable [CAC](../strategy/unit-economics.md), then expand.
