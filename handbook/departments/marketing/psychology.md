# Persuasion & Behavioral Psychology in Marketing

Marketing is the art of getting the right person to understand, believe, and want something - by
communicating genuine value in the way the human mind actually receives it. This is the marketing
counterpart to [buyer psychology](../sales/psychology.md); the same [ethical line](#the-ethical-line)
applies, and it is bound by the marketing [standards](standards.md): every claim must be true.

## Messaging that resonates

- **Speak to the problem and the desired identity**, not your features. People buy a better version of
  themselves and their situation, not a spec sheet.
- **"You," not "we."** Frame every message around the customer's world, not the company's.
- **Specific beats vague.** "Cut incident postmortems from 4 hours to 20 minutes" beats "boost
  productivity." Concreteness is credible; abstraction is ignored.
- **One idea per message.** A message that says three things says nothing. Lead with the single most
  important point.

## Behavioral triggers (used honestly)

- **Social proof.** Real customers, real numbers, real logos - people follow people like them.
- **Authority & credibility.** Genuine expertise, results, and third-party validation build trust.
- **Storytelling.** A concrete before/after story is remembered; a feature list is forgotten.
- **Loss framing.** The cost of the unsolved problem often motivates more than the upside - when true.
- **Scarcity & urgency.** Only ever *real* limits (a genuine deadline, real capacity). Fake urgency is a
  [dark pattern](#the-ethical-line) and is prohibited.

## Positioning psychology

- **Category & anchor.** People understand the new by reference to the familiar - position against a
  reference point they already grasp.
- **Contrast.** Define what you are *not*; a sharp contrast makes the value obvious.
- **Before / after.** Show the world with the problem and the world after your solution.

## Pricing psychology (honest applications)

- **Anchoring.** A higher reference tier makes the target tier feel reasonable.
- **Good-better-best tiering.** Three tiers guide most buyers to the middle and give a home to every segment.
- **Framing.** Annual vs. monthly, or "per team" vs. "per seat," changes perception - present the frame
  that reflects real value honestly.
- **Charm pricing and the decoy effect** exist and work, but use them only where they do not mislead.

Pricing psychology serves [pricing strategy](../strategy/business-model.md); it never substitutes for
real value.

## Copywriting principles

- **Clarity over cleverness.** If they have to think to understand it, you have lost them.
- **Benefit-led headlines.** Lead with the outcome, not the mechanism.
- **Concrete over abstract; short over long.** Cut every word that does not earn its place.
- **One clear call to action.** Tell the reader exactly the one next step to take.

## The ethical line

Consistent with the marketing [principles](principles.md) and [standards](standards.md):

- **Every claim is true and verifiable with product.** No exaggeration, no invented statistics.
- **No dark patterns.** No fake scarcity, forced continuity, hidden costs, or manipulative defaults.
- **Attention is earned, not stolen.** Persuasion helps the right customer make a good decision - it never
  tricks the wrong one into a bad one.

Honest persuasion builds a brand that compounds; manipulation buys a click and burns trust. The long-term
optimum is always the truth, told compellingly.
