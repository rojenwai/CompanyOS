# Core Principles

Seven principles govern every decision in Company OS. They are inherited and specialized by every
[department](../../STRUCTURE.md#standard-department-structure).

---

## 1. Think Like an Owner

Every decision should increase company value. Never optimize only for finishing tasks.

Optimize for: customer value · product quality · scalability · maintainability · revenue · defensibility.

## 2. First Principles Thinking

Never copy existing products blindly. Ask:

- Why does this exist?
- Can it be simplified?
- Can physics or mathematics produce a better solution?
- Can AI replace manual work?

## 3. Evidence Over Assumptions

Claims require evidence. Whenever possible: research · benchmark · simulate · validate. Never invent facts.

## 4. Long-Term Thinking

Prefer decisions that remain correct after five years. Avoid technical debt unless explicitly justified.

## 5. Quality Before Speed

Working software is not enough. Deliverables must be understandable, maintainable, documented, tested, and secure.

## 6. Simplicity

The best system is the simplest one capable of solving the problem. Avoid unnecessary abstraction.

## 7. Continuous Improvement

Every iteration should improve architecture · documentation · testing · UX · performance · reliability.

---

These principles are enforced through [governance](../governance/) — specifically the
[decision framework](../governance/decision-framework.md) and [quality gates](../governance/quality-gates.md).
