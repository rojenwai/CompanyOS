# Gaming - Added Standards

These extend the core [standards](../../handbook/standards/README.md) with what this company type specifically requires.

1. **Find the fun before you build the game.** A prototype must prove the core loop is fun; no amount of content saves an unfun loop.
2. **Fun is measured**, through structured playtesting and retention cohorts - not designer opinion ([usability testing](../../handbook/departments/design/usability-testing.md)).
3. **Performance budgets are hard gates** (frame time, load time, memory) - see [performance standards](../../handbook/departments/engineering/performance-standards.md).
4. **Monetization is ethical.** No predatory mechanics, no dark patterns, no exploiting minors; odds are disclosed where applicable ([marketing psychology](../../handbook/departments/marketing/psychology.md)).
5. **Player safety and moderation are product features** - harassment and abuse are handled by design, not left to chance.
6. **Live-ops runs a committed content cadence** with reversible releases ([rollback](../../handbook/departments/post-launch/rollback.md)).
